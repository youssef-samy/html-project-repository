The case inside your computer contains components which each perform different roles. The motherboard is a big, flat piece of silicon, while the CPU ( Central Processing Unit) is a motherboard-installed chip.

Motherboard

  • It maintains and allows communication among many of a system's electronic components, such as the central processing unit ( CPU) and memory, and provides connectors for other peripherals.
  • It is on the size of A4 paper, its color is green or gold. On which we can distinguish the following components:
    • ROM
    • Battery
    • RAM
    • Expansion Slots
    • Expansion Cards
    • Connectors
    • Chip Sets
    • Processor, on which a fan or a heat sink is mounted.
    • Power supply to convert the 200v ac to 3.3 v dc.
    • Clock, which generates series of pulses per second

Motherboard Basics

In addition to the CPU, the motherboard also has RAM (Random Access Memory) slots built in "heads." RAM is primarily used for fast storage and data retrieval. This is also likely that you can mount video monitor, computer network, and audio equipment.

Motherboard Architecture

A motherboard includes "traces" of copper which are thin threads of metal used by the board for transmitting information and electricity. Such traces are imprinted on the board, forming loops, thus "printed circuit board," or PCB.

CPU Basics

The CPU is your Computer's "brain." The Intel and AMD (Advanced Micro Devices) are Popular manufacturers. The CPU usually has a large heatsink and fan on it, in the case it is distinguishing from other components.

CPU Architecture

In the meantime a CPU is built on wafers at a manufacturing plant (a "fab"). Some wafers are CPU sheets . A CPU also includes millions, often billions, of transistors used to execute millions of instructions per second that are placed on the motherboard through the copper traces.
